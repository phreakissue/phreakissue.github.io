PHREAK is a bi-annual fashion, arts and culture publication, exploring the world of the unknown, underrated or overlooked fashion designers, image-makers and artists; operating from the underground, miles below the common current. PHREAK should not be confused with the word ‘freak’ (although the association is not entirely unwelcome).

PHREAKISSUE001 features photographer Amo Bagratuni’s captivating take on Armenia’s devastating and largely overlooked conflict and looks back on Riga’s Untamed Fashion Assembly — a riotous celebration of trashion that Andrew Logan frequented in the 90s. Then comes George Nebieridze’s nauseating visual tales of ordinary madness. Bárbara Sánchez-Kane tells PHREAK why she does not want to paint inside the “fucking lines”, and Adrien Flores — rogue dentist and jeweller—shares the tales that teeth have told him. PHREAK rewatches Andrzej Żuławski’s mind- bending movies through a new perspective and meets Yudai Ono and Yodea Williams Braham’s monstrous creatures. To top all of it off, PHREAK has compiled a Decameron of its own. But if Boccaccio wrote his book to amuse ladies afflicted by love, PHREAK intends to divert the clubsick — not the lovesick, proposing clubs as an ultimate form of togetherness, a social glue (and a numbing agent) that binds these phreaks together.
